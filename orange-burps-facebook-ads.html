Creative Direction for Concept 3

  • Variant A image: Dark/black background, large white quote text, 5 stars in orange/gold. Minimal. Feels like a real testimonial screenshot, not a polished ad.
  • Variant B image: Bold solid orange (#ff6600) background, clean white sans-serif type. Punchy tagline layout.
  • Font: Use a serif font (Georgia, Playfair Display) for the quote in Variant A to feel personal. Use a bold sans-serif (Montserrat, Inter) for Variant B.
  • Keep text under 20% of image to avoid Facebook's reduced delivery penalty.
  • Do NOT put the product bottle in these images. The goal is pattern interrupt — it should look like a post, not an ad.

A/B Testing Notes

  • Test Variant A vs B in the same ad set, equal budget split
  • Run for 5-7 days minimum before picking a winner
  • Variant A (quote-led) will likely get higher CTR
  • Variant B (feature-led) will likely get higher conversion rate
  • Kill the loser after 1,000 impressions if CPA is 2x+ the winner

Creative Direction for Concept 5

  • OPTION 1 — Real photo (recommended): Actually stage and photograph two nightstands. LEFT: messy, cluttered with antacid bottles, water, pillows stacked up. RIGHT: clean, just a book and maybe a plant. Shoot in warm, natural lighting. This is the highest-performing option because it looks real.
  • OPTION 2 — Flat lay: Overhead shot. Left half: scattered antacid bottles, pills, packaging. Right half: single Orange Burps bottle, clean surface. Divide with a thin orange line.
  • OPTION 3 — Illustrated/graphic: Clean split-screen illustration. Works if you can't do photography. Use the HTML mockup above as a starting point for a designer.
  • Image ratio: 1:1 (1080x1080) for feed. Also create 9:16 (1080x1920) for Stories/Reels placement.
  • Add a small "BEFORE | AFTER" label at top of the split image in small caps.

A/B Testing Notes

  • Test real photo vs. graphic version — real photos almost always win on Facebook
  • Test Variant A (long copy, story-driven) vs Variant B (short, punchy) in the same ad set
  • The "nightstand" image is the hero — if neither variant works, the image is the problem, not the copy
  • Consider a 15-second video version: camera pans across cluttered nightstand, hand sweeps everything into a trash can, places single Orange Burps bottle down
Concept 1

The Review Wall

Why it works: Social proof is the #1 conversion driver on Facebook. Real customer words beat branded copy every time. Carousel format lets each review be its own "mini-ad" and Facebook optimizes for which card gets the most clicks.

Creative Direction for Review Wall Carousel

  • Card design: Bold orange (#ff6600) background, white text. One review quote per card in large, readable font. Customer first name + "Verified Buyer" at bottom.
  • Card 1 (hook card): Instead of a review, use a big bold stat: "4.8 stars from [X] reviews" or "90% get relief for 6+ months"
  • Cards 2-6: One review each, pulled from your best ones. Prioritize reviews that mention: (a) skepticism about the name, (b) specific competitor products they stopped using, (c) time-based results ("6 months later...")
  • Final card: Product image + CTA: "$19.99 — Try It Risk-Free"
  • Keep each quote under 100 characters for readability at mobile size

Recommended Audience Targeting

Audience 1: Direct Interest (Start Here)

  • Interests: Acid reflux, GERD, Heartburn, Gastroesophageal reflux disease
  • Age: 30-65+
  • Gender: All (heartburn skews slightly male but don't exclude)
  • Exclude: People who already purchased (use pixel data)

Audience 2: Competitor Buyers

  • Interests: Tums, Pepcid, Prilosec, Nexium, Gaviscon, Zantac
  • Behaviors: Online shopping, Health and wellness buyers
  • Layer with: Natural health, Organic products, Dietary supplements

Audience 3: Lookalike (Highest ROI)

  • Source: Upload your customer purchase list or use pixel Purchase event
  • Lookalike %: Start at 1%, expand to 3% if CPA is good
  • This will outperform interest targeting once you have 100+ purchases tracked

Budget Recommendation

  • Daily budget per ad set: $20-30/day minimum (Facebook needs ~50 conversions/week to optimize)
  • Testing phase: Run all 3 concepts for 7 days, $20/day each = $420 total test
  • Kill criteria: If CPA is 3x+ your target after 1,000 impressions, kill it
  • Scale criteria: Winner gets 20% budget increase every 3 days
