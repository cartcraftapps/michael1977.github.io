Creative Direction for Concept 3

  • Variant A image: Dark/black background, large white quote text, 5 stars in orange/gold. Minimal. Feels like a real testimonial screenshot, not a polished ad.
  • Variant B image: Bold solid orange (#ff6600) background, clean white sans-serif type. Punchy tagline layout.
  • Font: Use a serif font (Georgia, Playfair Display) for the quote in Variant A to feel personal. Use a bold sans-serif (Montserrat, Inter) for Variant B.
  • Keep text under 20% of image to avoid Facebook's reduced delivery penalty.
  • Do NOT put the product bottle in these images. The goal is pattern interrupt — it should look like a post, not an ad.

A/B Testing Notes

  • Test Variant A vs B in the same ad set, equal budget split
  • Run for 5-7 days minimum before picking a winner
  • Variant A (quote-led) will likely get higher CTR
  • Variant B (feature-led) will likely get higher conversion rate
  • Kill the loser after 1,000 impressions if CPA is 2x+ the winner

  • Test Variant A (long copy, story-driven) vs Variant B (short, punchy) in the same ad set

  • Consider a 15-second video version: camera pans across cluttered nightstand, hand sweeps everything into a trash can, places single Orange Burps bottle down

Creative Direction for Review Wall

  • Carousel cards: Dark background (#1c1e21), white serif text (Georgia/Playfair Display), 5 orange stars above each quote. First name + "Verified Buyer" in small muted text below. Keep clean and minimal.
  • Hook card & CTA card: Orange (#ff6600) background, bold white sans-serif. These bookend the carousel.
  • Single image (Variant B): Dark background, 3-4 short quotes stacked with orange left borders. Stars at top, logo/price at bottom. Should feel like a screenshot of a reviews page, not a designed ad.
  • Key rule: Do NOT clean up the review language. "Silly named pill" is more believable than perfectly written copy. Keep it raw.
  • Image size: 1080x1080 per carousel card. Same for single image.

A/B Testing Notes

  • Test Carousel (Variant A) vs Single Image (Variant B) — carousel usually wins on CTR, single image can win on conversion rate
  • If carousel wins, test swapping individual cards to find which reviews drive the most clicks
  • The "Retired RN" card (Carla) will likely be the highest performer — authority + natural health angle
  • Consider rotating in fresh reviews every 2-3 weeks to combat ad fatigue

Recommended Audience Targeting

Audience 1: Direct Interest (Start Here)

  • Interests: Acid reflux, GERD, Heartburn, Gastroesophageal reflux disease
  • Age: 30-65+
  • Gender: All (heartburn skews slightly male but don't exclude)
  • Exclude: People who already purchased (use pixel data)

Audience 2: Competitor Buyers

  • Interests: Tums, Pepcid, Prilosec, Nexium, Gaviscon, Zantac
  • Behaviors: Online shopping, Health and wellness buyers
  • Layer with: Natural health, Organic products, Dietary supplements

Audience 3: Lookalike (Highest ROI)

  • Source: Upload your customer purchase list or use pixel Purchase event
  • Lookalike %: Start at 1%, expand to 3% if CPA is good
  • This will outperform interest targeting once you have 100+ purchases tracked

Budget Recommendation

  • Daily budget per ad set: $20-30/day minimum (Facebook needs ~50 conversions/week to optimize)
  • Testing phase: Run all 3 concepts for 7 days, $20/day each = $420 total test
  • Kill criteria: If CPA is 3x+ your target after 1,000 impressions, kill it
  • Scale criteria: Winner gets 20% budget increase every 3 days

Facebook Ad Policy Compliance Notes

All copy in this pack has been reviewed for FB ad policy compliance:

  • No fabricated testimonials — Every customer quote is pulled verbatim from real, verified reviews in the Orange Burps review export.
  • No unsubstantiated health claims — Removed "90% of users get relief for 6+ months" (would require clinical substantiation). Replaced with review counts and "results may vary."
  • No implied cure or treatment — Softened "Take for 20 days. That's it." to "A simple 20-day protocol." Supplement ads cannot imply a product cures, treats, or prevents a disease.
  • No medication-stopping directives — Replaced "Ditch the Daily Antacids" and "Ditch the Antacid Aisle" with neutral headlines. FB prohibits ads that tell users to stop prescribed medications.
  • No personal health assertions — Removed "just like you" and changed "your nightstand" to third-person framing. FB's personal attributes policy prohibits implying the viewer has a specific health condition.
  • "Results may vary" — Added to all variants with testimonial-based claims.

Required on your landing page (orangeburps.com):

*These statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.
